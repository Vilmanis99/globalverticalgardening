## *What Are Microgreens?*

*Microgreens are young seedlings of vegetables and herbs that offer a high concentration of nutrients and beneficial plant compounds. Their popularity as trendy superfoods continues to rise due to their rich vitamin and carotenoid profile.*

![microgreen sprouted mustard seeds linen mat close up](/images/microgreens-all-you-ever-wanted-to-know/microgreen-sprouted-mustard-seeds-linen-mat-close-up.jpg)

### *Defining Microgreens*

*Microgreens are the young greens of specific vegetables and herbs harvested just after the cotyledon leaves have developed. Typically, they are about 1-3 inches tall and are distinct from sprouts, which do not have leaves. Microgreens are ready for harvest within one to three weeks after germination, depending on the species. They are notable for their various colors and textures, adding both visual appeal and flavor to dishes.*

![micro](/images/microgreens-all-you-ever-wanted-to-know/micro.jpg)

### *Benefits and Nutrition*

*Microgreens pack a potent nutritional punch. Research suggests they contain higher levels of* ***vitamins*** *and* ***carotenoids****—often four to six times more—compared to their mature counterparts.* Carotenoids are pigments found in plants, giving yellow-orange color to fruits and green color to leafy vegetables. They're important for health, especially as precursors to vitamin A. Carotenoids are crucial in the protection of humans against various cancers and are effective(1)

Intake of these tiny greens has been strongly linked to cardiovascular health benefits. The study showed that as seeds germinate and sprout, carotenoid levels climb, making both sprouts and microgreens excellent sources:

- **Microgreens** contain between **1,510.1** and **4,073.5 μg** of carotenoids per gram of fresh weight—with amaranth at the high end and radish at the low end.
- **Sprouts** are more variable, ranging from **22.5 μg/g** in mung bean to **948.8 μg/g** in radish.

The dominant carotenoids identified include oxygenated types (lutein, neoxanthin, zeaxanthin) and carotenes (α- and β-carotene), with microgreens consistently richer than sprouts. Light exposure further boosts xanthophylls and β-carotene more than α-carotene. Since α- and β-carotene serve as provitamin A precursors, microgreens deliver roughly **21 times** more provitamin A than sprouts(2)

*These greens may include a variety of species, each with its own unique profile of* ***nutrients****. Common microgreen varieties, such as radish, kale, and cilantro, are rich in* ***vitamins*** *like vitamin C, vitamin E, and vitamin K. The concentrated levels of these nutrients contribute to the reputation of microgreens as a* ***superfood****. They not only enhance the flavor and aesthetic of meals but also boost nutritional intake.*

## *The Growing Process*

*When cultivating microgreens, one must pay careful attention to the sowing and germination stages, as well as the specific lighting and watering needs throughout the growth cycle.*

### *Sowing and Germination*

*Microgreens* ***seeds*** *require a well-prepared* ***soil*** *bed or a suitable soil-less growing medium for optimal germination. The* ***seeds*** *are densely sown in shallow trays and covered lightly with* ***soil*** *or a thin layer of vermiculite to maintain consistent moisture. Germination typically occurs within 2-3 days, during which the* ***garden*** *should be kept out of direct sunlight and at a moderate temperature.*

### *Lighting and Watering Needs*

*Once germinated, microgreens need ample light for photosynthesis and* ***growth****. They perform best with 12-16 hours of bright, indirect light per day. Watering should be gentle and minimal, keeping the* ***soil*** *moist but not waterlogged, to prevent mold growth and support healthy* ***sprouts****. Consistent monitoring ensures microgreens develop vibrant colors and robust flavors.*

## *Caring for Your Microgreens*

*Caring for microgreens involves managing their specific environment effectively. They thrive under certain conditions, and it's crucial to understand how to maintain them for optimal growth.*

![caring](/images/microgreens-all-you-ever-wanted-to-know/caring.jpg)

### *Optimal Environmental Requirements*

- ***Temperature:*** *Microgreens prefer a consistent temperature between* ***65°F and 75°F*** *(18°C-24°C).*
- ***Humidity:*** *Aim to keep the humidity level around* ***40% to 60%*** *to support growth and reduce mold risk.*
- ***Light:*** *Provide* ***12-16 hours*** *of light daily. This can be from a natural source or supplemented with grow lights for indoor gardening.*

### *Common Issues and Solutions*

- ***Mold and Mildew:***

  - ***Solution:*** *Improve air circulation around the plants, and ensure the growing medium is not too wet.*
- ***Leggy Seedlings:***

  - ***Solution:*** *This usually indicates insufficient light. Place the microgreens closer to the light source or extend the duration of exposure.*
- ***Yellowing:***

  - ***Solution:*** *Yellow leaves often suggest a nutrient deficiency. One can remedy this by applying a water-soluble fertilizer formulated for microgreens.*

*By adhering to these care requirements and promptly addressing common issues, one can successfully nurture microgreens and enjoy their benefits in indoor gardening.*

## *Harvest and Post-Harvest*

*Harvesting microgreens at the right time and storing them properly ensures maximum flavor, nutrient retention, and shelf life. This section provides detailed guidance on these essential steps to enjoying these nutrient-dense foods at their best.*

### *When to Harvest Microgreens*

*Microgreens are typically ready to harvest* ***7 to 14 days*** *after germination, once they have sprouted their first true leaves. It's crucial to harvest before the second true leaf stage for the peak flavor and nutrient content. One can witness their readiness by noting the spread of the first leaves and an even growth across the tray. Harvesting involves cutting the greens just above the soil line, ensuring no soil contamination occurs.*

![heap beet micro greens table](/images/microgreens-all-you-ever-wanted-to-know/heap-beet-micro-greens-table.jpg)

### *Storing and Extending Freshness*

*After harvesting, microgreens require immediate attention to maintain their freshness. They should be gently washed to remove soil and reduce microbial contamination. A salad spinner can be useful to remove excess water. For optimal storage, microgreens can be patted dry, then placed between two paper towels in a sealed container, and stored in the refrigerator. This method can extend their freshness for about* ***7 to 10 days****. Microgreens are delicate, so any excessive pressure or compact storage should be avoided to prevent bruising.*

This guide assists with identifying the visual cues for the perfect harvest moment. Also, methods for* [***harvesting microgreens***](https://mastermicrogreens.com/when-to-harvest-microgreens) *that optimize flavor and nutrition emphasize the importance of timely picking before the second true leaf develops.*

## *Microgreens vs Sprouts*

*Understanding the differences between microgreens and sprouts is essential for those interested in health, gardening, and nutrition. Both are young plants but they differ notably in growing methods and nutritional profiles.*

### *Differences in Growing Methods*

*Microgreens are often grown in a soil medium or a soil substitute, such as coconut coir, requiring sunlight for photosynthesis. They are typically harvested 7 to 21 days after germination, after their first true leaves develop. In contrast, sprouts are germinated seeds that grow in water and are usually ready to eat within 2 to 7 days. They do not need soil or light and are harvested before the true leaves emerge, consisting of the root, seed, and undeveloped leaves.*

### *Nutritional Comparison*

*When comparing their nutritional value, microgreens are thought to contain a higher concentration of nutrients compared to sprouts. This includes vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.* [***Microgreens***](https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/microgreens) *can provide a nutrient boost to a variety of meals. Sprouts, while also nutritious, have had concerns regarding food safety, as their moist growing conditions can be conducive to bacterial growth. It is important to handle and prepare sprouts carefully to reduce the risk of food contamination.*

## *Health and Safety Considerations*

*When cultivating and consuming microgreens, a focus on health and safety is crucial. Ensuring clean handling and preventing contamination are the two main pillars that safeguard microgreens from being a vector of food-borne illnesses.*

### *Handling and Hygiene*

*It is essential to practice good hygiene when handling microgreens. This includes frequent handwashing and using clean utensils and surfaces. The guidance provided by* [***Healthline***](https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/microgreens) *suggests that maintaining a clean environment is key to avoiding the introduction of pathogens. Those involved in cultivation and harvest must be diligent about cleanliness to ensure microgreens are safe for consumption.*

### *Preventing Contamination*

*Microgreens require strict measures to prevent contamination at every step, from seeding to packaging. The* [***University of Nevada, Reno***](https://extension.unr.edu/publication.aspx?PubID=3468) *emphasizes the importance of safe seed handling and climate control in the growing environment, as microgreens are often consumed raw. The following points are critical:*

- ***Seed Selection and Treatment:*** *Only use high-quality seeds from reputable sources. If required, treat seeds to eliminate potential pathogens.*
- ***Water Quality:*** *Employ only water that has been tested and shown to be free from harmful bacteria such as E. coli.*
- ***Harvesting Practices:*** *Harvest tools and containers should be sanitized regularly to prevent cross-contamination.*

*By following these procedures and keeping the safety of agricultural and food chemistry in mind, consumers and growers can enjoy microgreens with confidence in their quality and safety.*
