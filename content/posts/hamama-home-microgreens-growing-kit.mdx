## Hydroponic Growing Mats For Microgreens

Hydroponic growing mats is one medium that can be replaced with the soil or compost to grow microgreens. Coconut fiber is a typical example of hydroponic growing mats. Coconut fiber mat let's air pass through the roots and yet remain wet. After germination of seeds, the roots pass through the fiber to get water below. Also, you can reuse hydroponic growing mats after the first harvest and for several crops.

## How Do You Grow Super Greens With Hamama?

We have closed the cell about what you will get in your starter kit of hamama home microgreens growing kit, now let’s move to the things that you should have in the whole process of kitchen farming.

Let’s assume we have a starter kit from hamama and now it’s time to set up all the things for our first crop;

**#1** Please keep in mind, the place you have decided to grow your microgreens should have a controlled atmosphere like temperature, aeration, humidity, and light.

Note: Ideal temperature should be between 15 to 21 degrees C, although the warmer temperature will help speed up the growth cycle of microgreens.

**#2** Place the Hamama super greens tray and pure water in it till the “Fill Line.”

**#3** It is the time to unpack your desired seed quilt and place in the tray, remember you are following easy instructions that come with the growing kit/seed quilt pack. You are instructed to place coconut fiber mat in the bottom and soft press this mat for at least 10 to 15 seconds so that the fiber soak some water – for a proper supply to seeds.

**#4** You now can put a cover on the tray to stop light and let the process begin in the dark for 24 hours.

After 24 hours, you will see some progress; seeds now have started germination. You can use microgreens growing light with the timer to provide 12 hours of light supply and 12 hours of the dark – in case you have no natural source of light for microgreens.

**#5** After 3 to 4 days when greens start growing, you can pill-off the top paper cover.

**#6** In the total duration of growing superfood, you have to check water and the coconut mat should be wet – Overwatering your greens don’t harm as well as under-watering. You can also buy a spray bottle to keep them moist.

**#7** Most varieties of microgreens take 12 to 16 days to get ready for harvest. It is the time when you are going to cut the leaves with stems to use as your nutrition-rich food. You can use sharp sterilize scissors to cut them, and before you eat, wash them genteelly with warm water. If you want to use them later, then the best way is to wrap then in a moist towel and use Ziploc bag before storing it in the refrigerator.
