Decorating the dinner table with floral centerpieces has been a tradition for centuries. Some flowers are also used for garnishing the dishes on your dining table. Flowers have always been used to enhance the aesthetic sense of the dining table and dishes.

But hardly a handful of people know that certain flowers can be consumed as a main course too. Yes, you heard us right. Some flowers are edible as well.

 Many chefs use edible flowers to cook delicious, nutritious dishes. You can either eat fresh flowers or can even cook them to consume.

One can easily find edible flowers displaying on the shelves of grocery stores. Growing your edible flowers is much better and healthier than buying them from stores. By growing them yourselves, you get a whole new dimension of satisfaction and insurance that the growing flowers are 100% organic and free from harmful chemicals (fertilizers).

But before you learn how to grow edible flowers, first you need to know which flowers are edible and which are not.

## How To Identify Edible Flowers?

## Right Way To Prepare Germination Tray

✓    As your plan for growing edible flowers collects all the material needed for growing the edible flowers at your home.

✓    Once the material is collected, start preparing the germination trays for sowing the seeds. Prepare 1 germination tray for 1 plant.

✓    Add potting soil to germination trays and spray some amount of water onto them.

✓    Label the trays with the names of plants that bear the edible flowers to prevent mixing.

## Right Way Of Sowing The Seeds

✓    Always use healthy seeds for growing edible flowers.

✓    After soaking for 24 hours, place the seeds in the soil.

✓    Put a tiny amount of soil to cover the seeds.

✓    Spray some amount of water with the help of a spray bottle on the soil.

✓    Cover the germination tray with a dome.

✓    Remove the dome after 24 hours.

×   **Warning:** Do not water the soil until the seeds sprout.

✓    After the seedlings have emerged from the soil, place the germination tray in a location where the sunlight falls directly onto it.

## Transplanting The Seedlings Into The Pot

✓    The pots you are going to use for growing the edible flowers should have proper drainage.

✓    After merging the seedlings from the soil, remove the weaker seedlings from the tray. Keep the healthy and stronger seedlings. Now transfer the seedlings to the pots.

✓    Place the pots outdoors in the garden. There should be enough sunlight falling on pots because edible flowers grow best in full sun.

✓    Water the plants after 2 to 3 days. Do not let the soil dry out completely.

### Important Note

When the seedlings start growing into plants it is better not to use the pesticide. Because the pesticide will add some dangerous chemicals into the flowers. Which will not be good for your health. You have to scout on the plants to remove the pest by hand. Growing edible flowers is a very sensitive procedure. Because at the end you are going to consume the flowers. So, it's better to stay away from toxic chemicals.

## Harvesting

The best time for harvesting edible flowers is in the morning. It is because in the morning the edible flowers are turgid because of water content. The petals of the flowers that have been harvested in the morning will stick to each other less.

Harvest only those flowers which are completely opened. Keep harvesting the flowers to promote the growth of more flowers on the plant.

## Right Way Of Storing The Edible Flowers

✓    Store the edible flowers right after harvesting them. Different methods can be used to store edible flowers.

✓    You can store them in plastic containers. Keep the container away from sunlight.

✓    Before moving them into containers place them on paper towels. Paper towels will keep the edible flowers moist.

✓    The clean flowers can be stored directly. But the dirty flowers need to be cleaned before storing. The delicate leaves of edible flowers can be clean by dipping them into water.

✓    Oil and vinegar can be used as well for storing edible flowers.

#### ×   Warning: Pistils and pollens should be removed from the flowers before consumption. This is because some people can be allergic to pollen.

✓    Edible flowers can also be stored in ice cubes. These ice cubes then can be added to drinks for additional flavor.

## Precautions When Consuming Edible Flowers

You need to take care of some precautionary measures before deciding to eat edible flowers.

●     A person who has asthma or pollen allergy should not eat edible flowers.

●     Be very sure about the edible flowers you have bought from the market before consuming them.

●     it is safer to eat edible flowers as a food garnishing than eating them as a whole.

●     If you are planning to give edible flowers to children, introduce them into small quantities.

 ●     Never use pesticides when growing edible flowers at home.

●     If you are going to grow edible flowers with heavy pollen, use a face mask made of cotton.

●     If you want to add edible flowers to the salads or for decorating the cakes it is better to not purchase them from the florists and nurseries. It is because there is less chance that they did not spray the pesticides on them.
