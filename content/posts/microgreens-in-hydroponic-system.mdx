# What Is Hydroponic Microgreens System

Hydroponics means working with water,” in the Greek language. It is a water-based idea of growing plants. Instead of using soil, we utilize water in a hydroponic microgreens system.

 Microgreens in the hydroponic system live in an aquatic-based environment. As a result, they grow without soil. All nutrition and oxygen for plants come from water rather than soil.  That doesn’t mean that you don’t add fertilizers into your plants. For growing microgreens in the hydroponic system, nutrient-rich solutions are essential, which add through compost.

## Benefits of Hydroponic Microgreens System

### 1. Less Water

In a hydroponic microgreens system, you can reuse the water. As a result, you can save much water compared to regular soil planting.

### 2. Little Space Required

Microgreens in the hydroponic system don’t require much space. You can grow them in your garage, in a tiny container, or at the terrace.

### 3. No Soil

You don’t have to stress over the following soil all through your home or incidentally spilling a tray of soil on your rug.  You also won’t be worried about composting your waste. In this way, you can grow microgreens without soil.

### 4. Some Microgreens Grow Better Hydroponically

Amazingly there are some microgreens grow best in hydroponically compare to grown in the soil.

# Can You Reuse Hydroponic Water

A common question you may have is whether you can reuse hydroponic water. Then the answer is certainly! You can use it. It not only saves money on water use in your hydroponic system. It is ideal for recycling and using the water with the goal that you can work an increasingly productive.

Utilizing a hydroponic system is gainful because it permits you to recycle and reuse water. In this manner, you will decrease water utilization; as a result, it helps the environment and makes maintaining the hydroponic system cost-efficient.

These can be a shift from one harvest to the next. Most importantly, it is essential that you clean and sterilizes the water before you reuse it. It will minimize any risk of infection and damage to your cannabis plants. Reusing water in a hydroponic system can be challenging as the used water may carry different bacteria, viruses, and any diseases. These can damage the harvest. That is why it is necessary to sterilize the water.

Cleaning and purifying the water can minimize the infection risk. In addition, below are some main ways through which you can reuse hydroponic water

### 1. The Heating Process- Pasteurizing

To utilize the Heating Process, you need to quickly heat the water with heat exchangers therefore it swap the temperature among two fluids. The heat exchanger cools or heats the water that you need to in your hydroponic system. With the Pasteurizing process, you can add minerals in the water. But remember for consistent efficiency, you need to check that it’s clean.

### 2. The Ozone Sterilizing Process

When you infuse water using ozone, it means that you will be able to recycle water efficiently and effectively in your hydroponic system. It comes naturally from the sun, yet it can also be artificially created and used in the recycling and sterilization of hydroponic water.

### 3. The UV Process

UV disinfection is another compelling procedure for reusing and utilizing water in your hydroponic system. Chemicals should not add in it; thus, it is an appealing and natural strategy for disinfection that cannabis cultivators use.

In conclusion, let’s tell you that you can recycle and reuse the hydroponic water which is not only cost-efficient by saving water, but on the other hand it gives nutrient to the microgreens.
