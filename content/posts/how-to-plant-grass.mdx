## What Is The Best Time To Plant Grass?

You can grow grass fast and strong when planting season of the grass aligns with the seed’s active growth. Just like other plants, grass also varies with climate and growth. There are two types of grasses. One is cool-season grasses, and the other one is warm-season grasses.

Tall fescue, perennial ryegrass, and Kentucky bluegrass are considered to be cool-season grass, and the fastest way to grow grass is to have it in the late summer. Cool-season grass grows fastest in northern climates and in the transition zone where both cool and warm regions overlap.

Centipede grass, Zoysia, Bahiagrass, and Bermudagrass are warm-season grasses, and the fastest way to grow these grasses is the warmer temperatures of early summer. Planting a new lawn with warm-season grasses needs to be in southern as well as the western region.

Whether you have cool-season grass or warm-season grass region is very important. Above all, timing your seeding to grow grass fast is essential. The timing keeps seeds germinated and grows quickly.

Appropriate planning and timing let a wide range of seeds of grass to root well; as a result, it grows grass fast. The look of your lawn is depending on the type of grass, growing region, and weather conditions.

Your soil can be compacted beneath your grass if your lawn is much use area for you or your kids. If you prefer to walk play and party on your lawn you will find that your soil is compacted. As a result, you may lose your lush green grass.

Moreover, micro-organisms automatically develop in the soil and reduced any thatch. You can aerate your lawn with renting an aerating machine or by the handheld aerating tool.
