## What is a Compost Bag?

Compost bags are also known as compostable bags. These are **bags made of natural plant starch, and they do not release toxic material.** In short, the compost bags **break down easily compost bin via microbial activity to make compost.**

Although the work of the compost bags is to line up your countertop [compost bin,](https://www.globalverticalgardening.net/composting/what-do-you-need-to-start-a-compost-bin) they have different designs. **Some are biodegradable, while others are compostable.**

**Difference** between Biodegradable and Compostable Bags Today, the market is full of different eco-friendly bags for both home and industrial composting. However, most bags are termed as compostable. **Is it true that compostable bags are the same as biodegradable bags?** The answer is **NO**!

 **Compostable bags breaks down quickly** while set in specific conditions. However, these conditions are different for both home composting and industrial composting use. Compostable bags are **good for home use, especially in organic farming.**

On the other hand, **biodegradable bags break down with time**, while composting in a landfill. In short, biodegradable bags takes time to break, **but they will finally decompose to become soil.** Good for use in trash companies.

Although both biodegradable and compostable bags are perfect alternatives for standard plastics, it is advisable you use compost bags for your home use. According to [naturespath.com](https://www.naturespath.com/en-us/), these are the [differences of biodegradable and compostable bags.](https://www.naturespath.com/en-us/blog/whats-difference-biodegradable-compostable/)

## Are Compost Bags Compostable?

Compost bags are **100% compostable**. **This means when you throw your compost bags in your compost pile, it will change into compost within a short time.** On the contrary, a [biodegradable bag](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodegradable_bag) cannot change into compost if your throw it in your compost pile.

## Drawbacks of Compost Bags

One of the major drawback of compost bags is that tying off is bad. Similar to plastic bag, the work of compost bags is keeping your waste well. However, **tying off a compost bag** blocks the microbes from getting in the bag. **In turn, it takes longer to break**.

Second, to **prevent your compost bag from tear and wear**, you need to change it regularly. Based on the amount and type of waste, most people change their compost bags two to three times in a week. This means **you need to change your bag often**, if you produce more kitchen waste.

Furthermore, some bags do leaks moisture. Therefore, **you need to use such compost bags with paper towels to soak the drips**.
