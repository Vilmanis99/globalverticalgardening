Wheatgrass microgreens have been praised for decades — and for good reason. As more people seek natural ways to boost their immunity, fight inflammation, and feel energized, wheatgrass microgreens remain one of the most potent superfoods available. Whether you're adding them to smoothies, juices, or salads, these tiny greens deliver big results.

Let's explore exactly why wheatgrass microgreens are still worth growing and eating in 2025.

## 1.       High in Nutrients and Antioxidants

Fully matured wheatgrass is a perfect source of a couple of vitamins and minerals. It’s higher in vitamins A, C, and E and also has magnesium, iron, calcium, and acids. Considering that microgreens have 10 to 40 times the nutrients in fully matured food types, you can expect wheatgrass microgreens and sprouts to have more of these nutrients.

They also contain several vital antioxidants including the essential glutathione and vitamins C and E. These antioxidants help to find free radicals to shield body cells from damage, and also reduce oxidative stress. Based on some studies, it has vital that antioxidants may help prevent some conditions like cancer, heart disease, and arthritis.

## 2.       Helps to Fight Infections

A 2015 research found out that wheatgrass products can kill or halt the growth of certain infections. They are therefore best suited for treating antibiotic-resistant conditions or people allergic to certain antibiotics. Researchers experimented with a test tube and published the results that indicated that wheatgrass has antimicrobial properties that can help fight certain types of strep infections and some forms of Lactobacillus bacteria. This lactobacillus bacteria plays a significant role in many infections including dental-related. To keep the doctor away, it's not just the apple that you haveto eat. Wheatgrass microgreens may play the same role.

## 3.       Restores Alkalinity to the Blood

![wheat grass grow and drink](/images/nutritional-benefits-of-wheatgrass-microgreens/wheat-grass-grow-and-drink.jpg)

Cancer claims lives. In 2019 over 600,000 people died of cancer in the US alone. To live a cancer-free life, the first step is changing your diet and understandinghow cancer cells grow. They thrive in an acidic body, and keeping your body in a slightly alkaline state is key to preventing any developing of these cells leading to the deadly condition.

Some trustworthy medical sites state that a slightly alkaline body is key to preventing chronic low-grade metabolic acidosis,which can result inantibiotic-resistant cramping, bloating, headaches, and other health issues. Fortunately for those who consume wheatgrass microgreens and smoothies, the superfood is highly alkalizing.

## 4.       Helps Lower Cholesterol and Triglycerides

Studies conducted at the Sharma University of India, wheatgrass is essential for heart and blood vessel health. It can be usefulin treating hyperlipidemia. Moreover, it helps lower high cholesterol which is otherwise harmful to your heart health and also lowers triglyceride levels. One study was done on rabbits to examine the effects of wheatgrass. Three groups of ten rabbits each were put to the test.

One group received a high-fat diet, the other one a control diet, and the last one received a high-fat diet together with wheatgrass for more than ten weeks. Samples were taken from the animals for cholesterol analysis. The high-fat diet increased oxidative stress, hyperlipidemia and lowered vitamin C. The ones that consumed wheatgrass recorded significantly reduced MDA levels and higher vitamin C and glutathione levels.

## 5.       Wheatgrass Microgreens are a Source of Iron

Wheatgrass microgreens provide over 10 mg of iron per serving. Iron is essential for hundreds of enzymes and proteins in the human body. One of the most important roles of iron is helping to form the essential compound hemoglobin.

Hemoglobin helps to transport oxygen molecules through the blood. People who experience low iron levels tend to be anemic since their tissues lack enough oxygen. Enjoying a cup of wheatgrass smoothie made from sprouts or microgreens, or consuming them directly as salads will ensure your iron level is average.

## 6.       May Aid in Blood Sugar Regulations

High blood sugar can result in thirst, headaches, frequent urination or fatigue. If the condition lasts longer, there will be serious consequences like nerve damage, skin infection, and issues with vision. According to some animal studies, wheatgrass can help control blood sugar levels.

However, the research was done on rats where it was noticed that their blood sugar levels decreased significantly after 30 days of consuming wheatgrass. More studies may be needed to understand how it may affect humans, but the chances are that a cup of wheatgrass microgreens may be very helpful in blood sugar regulations.

## 7.       May Help in Type 2 Diabetes Prevention and Treatment

People have used wheatgrass traditionally as a treatment for type 2 diabetes, and scientists have found evidence that may be helpful. In a2016 study done on rats, it became clear that wheatgrass raised insulin levels and also lowered blood glucose.

Experts are convinced that inflammation plays a critical role in diabetes. By fighting inflammation, wheatgrass may also help people manage both diabetes and some other complications related to it. Such a valid reason you should roll out a wheatgrass microgreen diet.

According to a study done by Hippocrates Health Institute, wheatgrass has been used traditionally as a blood cleanser.

Many people tend to drink wheatgrass smoothie as a way to boost weight loss. Wheatgrass contains thylakoids which are tiny compartments found in plants containing chlorophyll.
