## The problem

Sometime around the second week of May the ants showed up everywhere. Not the small black indoor ones you can ignore for a while — these were the bigger garden ants, building soft conical mounds along the foundation of the house, into the cracks between the patio stones, and up under one corner of the wooden shed. We&apos;d wake up and there&apos;d be a line of them along the kitchen step.

I&apos;d been planning to leave them alone — ants are good for the soil, they aerate, they kill aphid larvae — but two of the mounds were directly against the house wall, and one was inside the coldframe where I was starting seedlings. That changed the calculation.

So I went to Depo (the Latvian hardware chain) and picked the first ant product on the shelf that wasn&apos;t a spray. I don&apos;t want sprays near the seedlings or the vegetable bed, and a powder you can sprinkle along the foundation made more sense than fumigating. €3.60 for a 100g pot. Latvian-language label, but the BROS branding is the same as the Polish brand you see across Eastern Europe.

## How I used it

The instructions on the pot were straightforward — sprinkle a thin line of powder along the area where the ants are active, don&apos;t water it in for the first few hours, and repeat after a week if needed.

What I actually did:

1. **Day 0 (evening)** — Walked the foundation perimeter and laid down a thin line of powder. The 100g pot is enough for about 30 metres if you don&apos;t overdo it. Added another small pile directly onto the two visible mounds.
2. **Day 0–3** — Left it alone. Didn&apos;t water it in. The forecast was dry which helped.
3. **Day 4** — Light rain. Re-applied a small amount onto the two mounds that were still showing activity. Bought a second pot from Depo because the first one was almost gone.

Nothing fancy. The whole job took maybe 20 minutes.

## The verdict

**It worked, but I'm not going to oversell it.** The mounds went away. The trails into the house went away. The colony is clearly weaker. But I still see the occasional ant on the patio two weeks in — they&apos;re not extinct, just under control.

For me, that&apos;s the right outcome. I didn&apos;t want every ant in Latvia dead — I wanted them out of the house wall and the coldframe. That happened. The garden ants are doing what they're supposed to do, just not in the places I needed them gone from.

**Would I buy it again:** Yes. Same product, same brand, this autumn before they tuck in for winter — and again next spring as preventive treatment.

**One honest caveat:** this is a single test on one type of ant in one specific situation. If you have a different species (carpenter ants, fire ants, pavement ants in different climate) the same product might behave very differently. The active ingredient matters more than the brand.

## What I'd try next

This worked well enough that I&apos;ll keep it as a routine, but a few things I want to test in the future:

- **Diatomaceous earth as a chemical-free alternative** — slower-acting, but worth knowing if it's enough for the mild cases
- **The same granule product, but applied in early April before the mounds appear** — preventive vs reactive
- **Boric acid bait stations** indoors for the few stragglers on the kitchen step — for when one or two ants come in but I don&apos;t want to treat the whole foundation again

Each of those will get its own field-test article when I run them.
