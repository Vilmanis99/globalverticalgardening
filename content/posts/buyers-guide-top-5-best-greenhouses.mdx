## Factors to consider when choosing the best greenhouse

**Size**Although this is obvious, you should always remember that your greenhouse requires some anchoring. So, make sure you buy a greenhouse that suits your area well. By this I mean the base and everything.

**Ventilation**Good air-flow is essential for your plants throughout their lifespan. However, apart from circulating air, vents manage greenhouse temperatures when the weather is hot. Because overheating is harmful to your plants, it is advisable you keep the door open for air intake.

**Weight**Besides being obvious, weight factors in many decisions. In most cases the sturdiness of a greenhouse is determined using its weight. However, if you are doing the assemblage alone, or managing the delivery, the best greenhouse for you is a lighter one. Nonetheless, ensure you have weighed the benefits and risks before purchase. And, always ensure you have anchored your greenhouse.

**Insulation**All greenhouses are made with insulating materials. But, you should have an additional boost if you live in an area with colder climate. The thinnest material is 4mm, and it lets more heat transfer to take place. Although at times the best solution is going for a thicker material, a double-layered or twin-walled sheet is the best solution. The added air padding brings a lot of in difference.
