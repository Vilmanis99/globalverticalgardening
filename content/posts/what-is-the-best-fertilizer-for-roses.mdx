## What Do Roses Need?

The foremost question that should come into your mind while thinking about the best fertilizer is that what does your rose actually needs to grow properly. After going through the nutritional needs of your rose plant, you’ll be able to answer questions like what is the best fertilizer for roses.

Here are the main nutritional elements that you should expect your rose plant to need:

- Nitrogen
- Phosphorus
- Potassium
- Other nutrients in minute quantities

Nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium are the main nutrients that any plant including your rose plant would need for proper growth. Each of these nutrients brings in a special advantage for the plant.

### What Nitrogen Provides Roses

![What is the best fertilizer for roses?](/images/what-is-the-best-fertilizer-for-roses/WhatsApp-Image-2020-08-20-at-12.19.28.jpeg)

Nitrogen is necessary for the plant as it is responsible for the healthy growth of leaves. In the rose plant, the provision of the required quantity of nitrogen would lead to more and better flowering as compared to the nitrogen-deprived plants. Similarly, the excess nitrogen is what you should not aim for as it can result in higher foliage reducing the chances of flowering in the rose plants.

### What Phosphorus Provides Roses

The use of phosphorus in any plant helps in strengthening its roots and the production of flowers. For the rose plant, it is actually a blessing. The higher the amount of phosphorus, the more fresh looking your roses would be. But keep in mind excess of everything is bad, so don’t overwhelm your rose plant with too much phosphorus as it can create a detrimental impact on your rose plant.

### What Potassium Provides Roses

You can consider potassium as the immune system for the plants. It helps them recover the attack of the insects or any other sort of damage that they face. So, the absence of potassium from the nutritional pool of your rose plant can make it an easy target for all the insects and diseases. Yellow leaf margins, poor development of the stem, and withering flowers could be the visible result of potassium deficiency in the rose plant.

### Other Essential Nutrients For Roses

Although, the above-mentioned nutrients are the basic nutrients that your rose plant would need some other essential nutrients may not be required in great quantities like that of the ones mentioned above. However, the presence of these nutrients is essential too. These nutrients include copper, iron, magnesium, boron, manganese, zinc, etc.

Make sure that these nutrients, which aren’t required in great quantities, should be provided to the plant in abundance as it can create other problems for your rose plant and you won’t like that. Won’t you?

## What Is The Best Fertilizer For Roses?

Now when you are aware of the nutritional requirement of your rose plant, you’ll be in a better position to sketch a clearer picture of your question, “what is the best fertilizer for roses?

The best fertilizer would contain all the above-mentioned nutrients in the right quantities to facilitate the plant in growth and flowering. Mostly, there are two main categories of fertilizers that you would come across while gardening for the rose plant or any other plant. These include:

- Organic fertilizers
- Inorganic fertilizers

Let’s discuss them in a bit detail:

### Organic Fertilizers Used For Roses

These fertilizers are the ones made from the animal or plant matter. With their green approach towards the environment, they must be given preference. These fertilizers can be suited to bring back the right texture for the soil and replenish the soil with healthy microbes.

### Inorganic Fertilizers Used For Roses

This is the second form of fertilizer, which is synthetic or chemical ones. They are easier to use as compared to the organic fertilizers and can be available for you at a lesser cost. However, the overall impact on the soil that the inorganic fertilizers would create is zero.

If you want to plant your rose plants in your lawn, it is advised that you should go for organic fertilizers as they are going to have a long-term impact and can make your rose flowers look awesome.

## Factors To Consider Before Choosing The Right Rrose Fertilizer

Here are some of the other factors to consider before choosing the right fertilizers.

### Salt Formation

Continuous use of the fertilizers can result in the creation of excessive salts in the soil. Which aren’t required by the plants? Sometimes, these salts can affect the flowers and even the entire plant growth. So, if you have such a situation at hand, you don’t need to go for the rose fertilizer as it won’t help. It will provide a means for further salt formation.

In this situation, all you need is proper soil drainage that won’t let these salts to accumulate at a place. Once you have resolved this issue only then you can expect to look for the best fertilizer for your rose plant.

### Environment

The environment in which you want to grow your rose plant is also important. While finding the answer to what is the best fertilizer for roses. Considering the season, humidity and the hotness of the weather is also important. Before choosing a particular fertilizer for your rose plant.

This is because some fertilizers are right for a particular environment and may not work efficiently in the other. So, you should be cautious about this fact before making the decision.
